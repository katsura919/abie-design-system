04  Voice & Tone

How Abie Speaks

Honest + Blunt

Says what's uncomfortable but true. No sugarcoating. No motivational empty calories. Reads like a smart friend who's been where you want to go.

Specific + Precise

Real numbers. Real sequences. Not "build an emergency fund" — "get to 6 months before making any major moves." Specificity is credibility.

Systems-Minded

Everything has a sequence, a lever, a bar to break. Frames problems as cages and readers as intelligent enough to break them.

The Tilde Rule ~ Always ~ Never —

Em-dashes (—) are never used in Abie Maxey copy. The tilde (~) is the brand separator. It appears in navigation labels, section subtitles, inline metadata, and anywhere a visual break is needed between two ideas. It reads warmer and less formal than an em-dash.

08  Do's & Don'ts

The Rules

✓ Do

  • Use ~ (tilde) as separator, never em-dash
  • Use font-black uppercase tracking-tighter for all headings
  • Mix serif italic accent words into sans headlines
  • Keep monospace text very small (9–11px) and uppercase
  • Use the warm off-white (#f9f5f2) as the primary canvas
  • Match primary color (#e3a99c) to accent words, hover states, and badges
  • Include Abie's personal voice ~ specific, honest, experiential
  • Put the most important text in the largest type size
  • Use foreground dark (#3a3a3a) for dark hero sections and cards
  • Keep images full-bleed with gradient overlays for readability

✕ Don't

  • Use em-dashes (—) anywhere in copy or UI
  • Use fonts outside the three-family system
  • Use fully saturated brand colors (no neon pink, no pure red)
  • Center-align body paragraphs or long text
  • Use motivational fluff or empty platitudes in copy
  • Make buttons anything other than pill-shaped
  • Use generic stock photography or travel-blogger aesthetics
  • Mix too many font weights ~ stick to 400 and 900
  • Use dark mode colors in light mode contexts or vice versa
  • Add drop shadows to UI elements ~ use border and bg instead
